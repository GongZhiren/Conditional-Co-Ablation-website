The one-sentence version

Importance is conditional, not intrinsic

Interpretability scores a component by ablating it in isolation. That view quietly breaks whenever a circuit is redundant: remove a primary component and a dormant backup takes over, so the primary looks unimportant (the model repaired the damage) and the backup looks unimportant too (it was silent all along). A single-ablation score misreads both sides of the redundancy — and every tool built on it (attribution, knockout, pruning) inherits the error.

CoAx asks a different question: once the primary circuit is removed, how much does each remaining unit's ablation effect grow? A backup has little effect alone but a large conditional effect. That single shift turns the blind spot into a signal.

First-order saliency ranks the backups near the bottom (they are dormant, so ablating one alone barely moves the logits). Conditional co-ablation ranks the same heads at the top. Same heads, two scores — the inversion CoAx exploits.

The method

One equation: conditional growth

Read every ablation effect in the output distribution's own (Fisher) metric on the clean top-r logits. Let \(\mathcal{E}(\delta z_u \mid S)\) be the mean energy of ablating unit \(u\) after a primary seed \(S\) has been ablated. The CoAx score is the growth of that energy under conditioning:

$$\operatorname{comp}_u(S)\;=\;\underbrace{\mathcal{E}\!\big(\delta z_u \mid S\big)}_{\text{effect once }S\text{ is gone}}\;-\;\underbrace{\mathcal{E}\!\big(\delta z_u \mid \varnothing\big)}_{\text{effect alone (first order)}}$$

Why it is blind-spot-proof

A dormant backup and a truly inert head look identical to any score read off the clean model — both are silent. CoAx separates them: the backup's effect grows once its primary is gone (\(\operatorname{comp}>0\)); the inert head's does not (\(\operatorname{comp}=0\)). This is a provable gap, not a heuristic.

Why it is cheap

The score is label-free and gradient-free: it needs one clean pass, one seed-ablated pass, and one pass per candidate — \(O(\#\text{heads})\) forwards, the same order as a single-ablation scan, and far below the \(O(\#\text{heads}^2)\) of enumerating interactions.

The primary seed can come from any first-order method (attribution patching, EAP-IG, AtP*) or a manual analysis. CoAx completes that circuit by returning the backups it hides.
